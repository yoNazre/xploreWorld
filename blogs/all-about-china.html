There are more than 200 Airports and more than 2000 seaports belong to China.

China's official name is the People's Republic of China.

China stands 4 on tourism ranking by UNWTO as it attracts more than 62 million people every year and the numbers are keep growing.

Political Geography

China is located in East Asia, China shares a border with 14 countries which is about 22000 Kilometres, It is the world's longest land border.

Physical Geography

China is huge country, It has a wide geography division but it’s mostly green in the east and desert, rocky in the west. On the east side of China and the coasts, It has the heavily populated alluvial plains that are generally flat, with numerous spots for shipping and harbors and beaches with amazing looking once like Panji Red Beach that blossoms every autumn. And in a little bit in the north, at the Jilin province and Heilongjiang and half of Inner Mongolia along the borders of Russia and North Korea and you hit the coldest spot in all of China excluding Himalayan region.


Every year the city of Harbin named the “Ice City”, has a huge ice sculpting festival that draws in millions of tourists in the winter months and upper side of it, that area was commonly referred to as Manchuria in the past named after Manchu people, which is where the Fu Manchu mustache gets its name from. A little in the west, at rest of the Inner Mongolian autonomous region, which is dominated by Mongolian plateau, at highland consisting of dry steps, hills, and Gobi desert. Little more at west you have reached the largest subdivisions in China-The Xinjiang Uyghur autonomous region.


This is basically the wild west of China, at over 1.5 square kilometers, only 5% of the area is fit for human habitation. This area is unique, rocky, rugged mountains and loaded with oil, making it China’s largest gas-producing regionA little south and you reach the strange Taklamakan desert or the Cold desert, this huge the basin of shifting and dunes is almost completely surrounded by the snow-capped mountains, trapping in the frigid winds, however, it still lies in the rain shadow zone, so it rarely receives any precipitation. In the south and then we reach the most notable autonomous region-the Tibetan plateau, as the highest region on earth with an average elevation of nearly about 5000 meters/16000 feet, this the area is situated on the Himalayan Mountains, the tallest mountain range on the planet, and the tallest mountain in the world- Mount Everest- straddles the border with Nepal.


LAKE : Qinghai Lake – The largest lake of China

Then we get the southeast by the provinces of Yunnan, Guangxi, and Guangdong as the warmest region in the country. These areas are some of the most picturesque rock forests and eroded mountain landscapes with the terraced rice paddies that have literally usurped the entire surface area of hills and mountains.


This place is full of Snub-nosed monkey. When you head inland, you reach the rural area, and you can encounter the vast network of rivers and creeks that irrigate the endless crop fields that never end, with the occasional pine and bamboo forest.


China is full of beautiful scenery unmatched anywhere else in the world like outstanding Rainbow mountains Danxia, Thousand island lake, Hangzhou, the setting for ‘Avtar’ was inspired by the Zhangjiajie National Park.


Demographics

China is the world’s most populous country with a population of 1.4 Billion around 19% of the entire world’s population about 1 out of every 5 people on the planet is Chinese.

  • In China, they have traffic jams that can last for many days on 50 lane- Highways.
  • In China, even if you are one in a million, there are still 1400 people exactly just like you. China is packed, However with the colossal population comes an endlessly broad spectrum of culture, tradition people groups, customs, and lifestyles.
  • China officially recognizes about 56 different ethnic people groups that inhabit the entire country. At about 90%, the largest majority of the population identifies as Han Chinese, 10% other ethnic groups and the subdivision group, the largest ones being the Zhuang, the Hui, the Manchu, and the Uyghur.

FUN FACT

There are actually about twice as many Mongols living in China than therein Mongolia itself. They actually write in the traditional vertical Mongol script.


When it comes to the population, you need to know about the imaginary Heihe-Tangchong line. This is a line drawn diagonally from the northern city of Heihe all the way to Tengchong in the south, and about 94% of the entire country’s population lives east of this line.


Official Language :

The official language of China in Mandarin or Putonghua, however, as mentioned before, regional languages exist and they are even allowed to publish and utilize their own script in public. You can find the largest linguistic contrast in autonomous regions.

The Zhuang language is spoken in the Guanxi autonomous region, and actually closer to the Thai and Lao language with some mutual understanding, the problem with that is it’s written in the Latin alphabet. The Uyghurs of the north write in Arabic based writing system, which makes China a little diverse country.

Cantonese: One of the most widely known dialects-Cantonese-spoken mostly in the south in the Guangdong province. They all still use the same Chinese character writing the system, so even you don’t understand each other, you can still communicate with writing.


Culture :

There are too many things, the Chinese have an incredibly long history of vibrant, exuberating customs, traditions, dynasties, discoveries, inventions, wars, alliances, art, building, food, apparel, policies and beliefs and so on.

Chinese have a very hard-working diligent type of social construct that looms over the entire population.


Students :

Students can typically spend about 12 hours a day on schoolwork and studying in order to pass college entrance exams, which is a big deal there. And they love practicing English, in fact, there are actually more people in China that speak English as a second language than there are in the United Kingdom (UK).

Some children that show signs of being gifted in certain academic, athletic and artistic progress are even selected by the state to undergo training at specific academics that cater to targeting those skills.


Ruling :

The the country operates under a one-party socialist state, YES China is communist but indifferent manner like the ruling party is called “Communist Party”, and they do endorse some of the democratic centralist principles that Lenin proposed, and they do advocate some semi-communist ideologies but in all reasonable definitions, China is not a full-on “communist” country.

After the 70’s, policies were relaxed, and China opened up a more free- market economy. Nonetheless, there are still restrictions on the press, access to the internet although VPNs and proxy servers are not uncommon to avoid that problem, freedom of assemble and even freedom of religion.


Friendly countries of China

China has diplomatic relations with almost every single country in the world, except some that either never really had the time meet up, or the few that recognize Taiwan sovereignty over the People’s Republic.
