How China Became the World’s Manufacturing Hub?

Aug 12, 2014 by Nazre

A Bit History

China got Independence in 1 October 1949, China was not rich from the beginning. It took time to china to appear on the world economy.

At the time when Japan and Hong Kong giving competition to European countries in technology, The Chinese people struggling with a shortage of food supplies, cheap clothing, inadequate housing. And the service sector was also so poor, all of these things embarrassed china globally.

So, How did China Develop?

How did china become manufacturing hub for the world?

In 1978, Chinese Economic reform came into existence by, China's paramount leader, Deng Xiaoping. He started the idea of “Socialism with Chinese Characteristics”. It allows private businesses, private ownership, and introduced a dual price system in which state-owned enterprises could sell products at a higher market price after a government planned quota was reached .

For the first time after Independence, the country is open to investment through the establishment of special economic zones. Mr. Deng introduced many reforms such as large scale privatization of state owned-enterprises to a large extent, Mr. Deng’s plan has been a success. Over the past four decades, China has achieved the fastest sustained expansion by a major economy in history. China's gross domestic products have risen at the speed of 10X.

And become the world's second-biggest economy, China has lifted more than 800 million people out of poverty.

In today's world, China is the first preference for establishing its manufacturing units, Apple, Microsoft, Canon, Samsung and Sony are some of them.

How China Is Becoming Self Dependent In Every Field?

China has manufactured almost everything for the world but still, China is not 100% self-dependent. But the scenario is changing very fast pace. China is moving towards manufacturing each and everything that uses in Chinese products. From microchips to aircraft everything. They are running so many mega projects to achieve their goal.


Can China produce its own high end chip?

Let’s find out: China spends more than 230 billion dollars in a year for importing microchips, even more than it spends on petroleum and it is China’s largest imports.

In 2016, China made more than 1.2 billion smartphones and became the world's largest producer & consumer of mobile phones but the crucial chips on which the phones run all have to be imported but the scenario is changing. China is building the fastest 5G chips and the size of this chip would be half of one square centimeter, It is consists of 1 to 10 billion transistors, The team is working on it very efficiently and soon they will be able to do so.

In recently December 2019, it took a major decision to remove all foreign computer equipment from there schools, Govt. offices etc. that can hamper billions of manufacturing trade. All computer screens, processors, every foreign hardware and software will be removed within three years under 3-5-2 plan.

In 2020 they will replace 30% hardware

1n 2050, 50% and the remaining 20% in 2022.

China's chip manufacturers looking for breakthroughs in many fields including core chips that support high-speed railway operations and electric vehicles like the insulated gate bipolar transistors IGBT. The new 5G era is coming.


Can China produce its own Aircrafts?

Aviation Industry: China has very busy skies and will be busier, there are currently 3000 aircraft flying more than 1.33 passengers every day. Most of these aircraft are manufactured in Europe or America but it is set for a gradual change. The expansion of China's internal aviation market over the next 20 years will see the number of civil aircraft needed to rise over 6000. This huge market has prompted Chinese engineers to design and build C919. It takes more than one million components to build a c919 and each must be made to the highest standard demanded by the world aviation industry.

After the extensive testing in-flight stimulator (Iron Bird), the c919's flight control system achieves a faultless level performance on its first real test flight, in the future, c919 will be a key part of the Chinese air transport system.


The mid-sized passenger jet will not only benefit travel across the nation and other countries and regions to which it exports but its manufacture will support more than 200 enterprises and 200000 skilled workers in the research and development. It has taken just 30 years for China to transform from an industrial backwater to the world's largest manufacturer.


Can China produce its own Mega Container Ships?

Chinese shipyard is 399 meters long, it will rank with the biggest in the world when fully loaded with containers it can sail for 40 days uninterrupted. This is the first time a container ship of this size with a capacity of 20,000 standard containers has been built in China. For Engineers precision is the greatest challenge in building this giant, they must work in all areas to a margin of error of fewer than 5 millimeters. The heart of the shipyard is this giant dock, the size of seven football fields. The dock is the yard's most valuable resource so efficiency dictates that each vessel should occupy it for the shortest possible time to speed the building process the various section of the ship are put together in modules away from the main dock. The original design of the ship splits it into 399 units for modules that can be assembled separately and simultaneously at various sites around the yard. These are then brought together for final assembly in the dock.

Although broken down into 399 modules each weighs many dozens of tons and moving them around is not easy, One of the largest modules the bridge and superstructure is ready to be hoisted into position, it weighs over 600 tones, it will be lifted by two huge gantry cranes that themselves weigh 1,200 tons in total, the real difficulty is not in the lifting but how to install it on the deck to the nearest millimeter it requires the precise coordination of dozens of skilled workers. In a few months’ time the Ship will plow across the oceans loaded with the treasures and trinkets affordable goods that have benefited people from Atlanta to Zanzibar.


Can China Generate its own Power?

China consumes nearly 6 trillion kilowatt-hours of power per year to provide this by thermal power plants alone it will put huge pressure on the environment and reduction of carbon emission is a key goal under the Paris climate agreement 2016 so the nuclear power is one way to achieve this while keeping the lights on. Though nuclear power has zero carbon emissions it does have other major safety issues, the engineers building China's new nuclear power stations must ensure safety cannot be compromised.

The 10,000 square meter factory in Shanghai is making the latest generation of nuclear power units by the highest safety standards in the world. The factory can build the equipment to fit out six nuclear power plants per year if all are put into operation annual coal consumption will reduce by at least 13.8 million tonnes. At the same time, a wide range of projects is being undertaken to provide diverse energy sources China's manufacturing industry is reliant upon.


Can China produce its own Automobile?

In the last 3 decades, China has become the largest producer and consumer of automobiles in the world, the rapid rise in the huge number of cars has also raised issues of energy consumption and pollution but now the situation is changing.

The factories are producing electric vehicles on a very large scale, making new energy vehicles are changing the traditional concepts of automobile manufacturing high integration and modularization of components has become the trend with its global lead in battery technology this enterprise has shifted the focus of its business to new energy vehicles and become a major supplier of electric busses providing new energy vehicles for more than 200 cities in 48 countries. Besides batteries, other core components of new energy vehicles are becoming a battleground for global producers. New technological breakthroughs have made electric vehicles increasingly effective and practical, this is an opportunity but also a challenge not only in terms of production technology but also in our collective way of thinking.


Can China produce its own manufacturing robots?

During the first stage of development in China's manufacturing industry-low, labor costs and product prices provided China with its competitive edge in the global market now this manufacturing industry is undergoing a major transformation and upgrade. In future manufacturing processes that are dull and repetitive will be handled by robots leaving humans to supervise or engage in more creative work.

There is a center for robotics development in northeast China, where they design robots for a variety of different roles, they will be at the cutting edge of the production lines of the future. The robotics industry in China is expanding by 20% year-on-year. A new production line comes into service its robots building robots, a master robot selects the designated parts on the shelf and transfers the parts to their correct positions on the flow line through automatic guided vehicles AGV then robots arms assemble the parts. A new robotic era is on the way.


Can China create its own GPS system?

Our modern world has become reliant on global positioning satellites for almost every activity whether, for commerce or pleasure land, sea, and air all are equally indebted to the service. China is planning on expanding BeiDou with another 35 satellites over the next 2 decades. The potential of the BeiDou navigation satellite system is continuously increasing, shortly wherever you are no matter day or night you and your smartphone will be under its watchful eye.


Can China make its own high-speed railway system?

The train is a part of a new series of high-speed trains called Fùxīng (复兴) which literally means rejuvenation.

In a few weeks, this new series of high-speed trains will take to the tracks for real as the world's fastest, journey time between Beijing to Shanghai will be cut to 4.5 hours as the rejuvenation series roll-out across the whole network. China will usher in a new high-speed era. in Chongqing city, northeast China, the birthplace of China's heavy industry a brand new factory has been commissioned the first batch of the rejuvenation series is being assembled they are wider, taller and longer than the previous generation of high-speed trains but their energy consumption is 15% lower the trains are manufactured to uniform standards trains of the same time produced by the other factories will all be compatible with each other even their components are compatible which will help reduce running costs.

China has the world's most complex natural environment the new trains will face extreme cold, heat, and sandstorms amongst other challenges.

China has 60% of the world's high-speed track which has carried more than seven billion passengers, with higher efficiency, longer service life, and higher energy efficiency China's high-speed trains are beginning to become the new standard for high-speed train travel.


Can China Compete for the World In Clothing Industry?

Even the most traditional manufacturing industry in China is undergoing its own information revolution, everything looks the same at a distance skilled workers, exclusive fabrics, and traditional machines but the appearance deceive.


Ms. Liu Yili left her job with a world top 500 company to come and work in China for two years ago. She said: Today, we can make personalized products based on data.


Weaving machines were key to the industrial revolution in the 18th and 19th centuries, automation reduces the cost of textile production after Henry Ford invented the assembly line, and manufacturing became even more efficient tens of thousands of products can be produced quickly and uniformly, manufacturing cost further dropped but at the same time individuality was lost.

But the situation is changing now each outfit produced in this Chinese factory is tailored to an individual customer.

Data is what drives this every order goes into the computer system from the internet and the system generates it as an independent code, this code will guide workers on the production line. Although the 300 production steps are the same the codes direct the machinists as to the different materials and production methods.


Ms. Liu Lili (Manager of the garment factory) states that: "On a production line, we can produce thousands of different products, for example, Men's wear, Children's wear. We can do all of these on a single production line".


Ten years ago the clothes turned out on this line were mass-produced now they are individually customized and tailored to each client each order will be turned around in a week, inventory stock is a thing of the past. This is bespoke tailoring for the mass market, everything has changed and yet is somehow the same just better, faster and more efficient.


Nutshell

A new way of manufacturing is coming more and more devices are connected to the internet, robots respond directly to incoming orders the emphasis is moving from a pure speed of reaction to speed+quality. This is the new made in China, a future of intelligent manufacturing, this is the future on which China is staking its industrial dream. China has already announced “Made In China 2025” With it, China aims to move away from being the "world factory" (producing cheap, low-quality goods due to lower labor costs) and move to produce higher-value products and services.
